BPH Treatment

UroLift® System Treatment

A minimally invasive solution for Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) — proven to relieve symptoms without cutting, heating, or removing prostate tissue.

How It Works

Four Steps to Relief

01
Enlarged Prostate
The prostate grows and blocks the urethra, causing lower urinary tract symptoms.
02
Implants Placed
Tiny UroLift® implants lift and hold enlarged prostate tissue away from the urethra.
03
Urethra Opened
The blocked urethra is restored to normal flow — no tissue removed.
04
Rapid Recovery
Most patients go home same day, without a catheter, and resume daily activities quickly.
No Cutting or Heating
The only BPH procedure that lifts and holds tissue without removal, heat, or incisions.
Preserves Sexual Function
Clinical studies confirm UroLift® does not cause new, sustained erectile or ejaculatory dysfunction.
Proven Quality of Life
Demonstrated significantly greater quality-of-life improvement than that reported for medications.
Rapid Symptom Relief
Most patients experience meaningful improvement within weeks of the procedure.
Get Off Medication
An earlier treatment option that can eliminate the need for ongoing BPH medications.
Office or Outpatient
Typically performed under local anesthesia in a physician's office or ambulatory surgery center.

Am I a Good Candidate?

You may be a candidate for UroLift® if you are:

  • Male, age 45 or older
  • Experiencing symptoms of BPH (frequent urination, weak stream, urgency)
  • Seeking an alternative to medication or more invasive surgery

Note: If you have a known allergy to nickel, titanium, or stainless steel, discuss this with your doctor before proceeding.

Measure Your BPH Symptoms

The International Prostate Symptom Score (IPSS) questionnaire can help you and your physician measure the severity of your symptoms and track improvement over time.

Take the IPSS Quiz ↗

How Does It Compare?

Unlike TURP, laser surgery, or medication, the UroLift® System is the only BPH procedure shown not to cause new and lasting sexual dysfunction. Speak with Dr. Choe or Dr. Lee to see if UroLift® is right for you.

What is BPH?

Enlarged Prostate — The Leading Reason Men Visit a Urologist

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) causes the prostate to enlarge and press on the urethra, restricting urine flow. Symptoms include frequent urination, weak stream, urgency, and nighttime bathroom trips. You can measure the severity of your symptoms using the International Prostate Symptom Score (IPSS) questionnaire.

01
Enlarged Prostate
The prostate grows and blocks the urethra, causing lower urinary tract symptoms.
02
Tiny Implants Placed
Small UroLift® implants are placed to lift and hold enlarged prostate tissue away from the urethra.
03
Urethra Opened
The blocked urethra is opened, restoring normal urine flow without removing tissue.
04
Rapid Recovery
Patients typically go home same day, without a catheter, and resume daily activities quickly.
