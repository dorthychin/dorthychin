Before Your Visit

Appointment Preparation

New Patients

  • Arrive 15 minutes early to complete paperwork
  • Bring your photo ID and insurance card
  • HMO patients: bring your visit authorization
  • Bring any relevant labs or test results
  • If possible, bring completed new patient forms
  • Notify us of any insurance changes prior to your visit

Returning Patients

  • Bring results of any ordered labs or tests from prior visits
  • Ensure your HMO referral/authorization is current
  • Notify us of any medication or insurance changes

Uroflow Preparation

If you are scheduled for a uroflow test:

  • Begin drinking water approximately one hour before your appointment
  • Arrive with a full bladder
  • If the urge is not yet strong upon arrival, continue drinking water in the waiting room until you are ready

Billing & Insurance

PPO & Major Insurance

We accept most major medical insurances and PPOs. If you are unsure whether your plan is accepted, please call our office before your appointment and we will verify your coverage.

HMO Referral Policy

Patients requiring referrals from their medical group are responsible for obtaining authorization from their PCP for first-time visits and when returning after being dismissed from follow-up. Patients seen without a valid referral will be held responsible for any charges incurred on that date of service.

Patient Responsibility

It is the patient's responsibility to ensure eligibility with their insurance and, when applicable, with their HMO. We will make every effort to verify eligibility, but cannot bill insurance when changes have been made without prior notification.

BPH Treatment

UroLift® System Treatment

A minimally invasive solution for Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH) — proven to relieve symptoms without cutting, heating, or removing prostate tissue.

How It Works

Four Steps to Relief

01
Enlarged Prostate
The prostate grows and blocks the urethra, causing lower urinary tract symptoms.
02
Implants Placed
Tiny UroLift® implants lift and hold enlarged prostate tissue away from the urethra.
03
Urethra Opened
The blocked urethra is restored to normal flow — no tissue removed.
04
Rapid Recovery
Most patients go home same day, without a catheter, and resume daily activities quickly.
No Cutting or Heating
The only BPH procedure that lifts and holds tissue without removal, heat, or incisions.
Preserves Sexual Function
Clinical studies confirm UroLift® does not cause new, sustained erectile or ejaculatory dysfunction.
Proven Quality of Life
Demonstrated significantly greater quality-of-life improvement than that reported for medications.
Rapid Symptom Relief
Most patients experience meaningful improvement within weeks of the procedure.
Get Off Medication
An earlier treatment option that can eliminate the need for ongoing BPH medications.
Office or Outpatient
Typically performed under local anesthesia in a physician's office or ambulatory surgery center.

Am I a Good Candidate?

You may be a candidate for UroLift® if you are:

  • Male, age 45 or older
  • Experiencing symptoms of BPH (frequent urination, weak stream, urgency)
  • Seeking an alternative to medication or more invasive surgery

Note: If you have a known allergy to nickel, titanium, or stainless steel, discuss this with your doctor before proceeding.

How Does It Compare?

Unlike TURP, laser surgery, or medication, the UroLift® System is the only BPH procedure shown not to cause new and lasting sexual dysfunction. Speak with Dr. Choe or Dr. Lee to see if UroLift® is right for you.
